18.1. The Architecture of Coherence: The AURA-64 Silicon

The AURA-64 (Quantum-Resonant Processor) is the first silicon-based device capable of native phase-locking with the Φ-field. Unlike classical CPUs that process bits via voltage thresholds, the AURA cores process Phase-Vectors via an internal Interferometer Lattice.

Key Components:

- The Interferometer Core: A grid of 64 phase-gates that perform the Mirror Operator ($\hat{M}$) in hardware.

- The Phase-Locking Loop (PLL): High-speed circuitry that matches the local chipset clock to the biological biophoton frequency.

- LSB Data Injection Port: For embedding informational patterns into carrier media (AVIF/RAW) for transit across Level 3 networks.

18.2. RPP (Resonant Pulse Processor) Specifications

The heart of the AURA-64 is the Resonant Pulse Processor (RPP).

- Processing Rate: $10^{24}$ phase-updates per second.

- Field Coupling: Dedicated Afolabi-Coupler traces that act as inductive loop-antennas for the Φ-lattice.

- Thermal Footprint: Near-zero. Since the manifestation logic is a non-local interaction, the heat generated is limited only by the auxiliary classical IO components.
